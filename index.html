Economic Thesis Paper

Economic Thesis (NC Counties Regression): Modeled 100 North Carolina counties (2012–16) and found that physical inactivity and excess drinking are the strongest predictors of poor self‑reported health. ​

Close

Leveraged County Health Rankings data across 100 North Carolina counties (2012–2016) to model how key behaviors—physical inactivity, obesity, and excess drinking—drive self‑reported health. Built an Excel regression controlling for demographics, socio‑economics, and county resources, revealing that inactivity has the largest negative effect on both physical and mental health, while heavy drinking disproportionately worsens mental well‑being. Concludes with data‑backed recommendations for increasing exercise opportunities and limiting alcohol consumption. ​
